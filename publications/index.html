    Performance Analysis of GLRT-Based SIMO-OFDM Demodulation With Pilot-Aided Channel Estimation
    Liao, Jy-Chin, Chen, Wei-Chang, and Chung, Char-Dir
    IEEE Systems Journal 2022
    Abs HTML

    Pilot symbols are commonly inserted into uniformly interleaved subcarriers to aid channel estimation, thereby facilitating data demodulation in practical orthogonal frequency-division multiplexing (OFDM) systems operating over block dispersive channels where the channel frequency response (CFR) remains static only over a few blocks. Aided by uniformly interleaved pilot symbols, the direct least-square (LS) CFR estimation can enable reliable symbol-by-symbol data demodulation within the same OFDM block in the absence of channel state information, based on the principle of generalized likelihood ratio test (GLRT). Such GLRT-based OFDM can be easily incorporated with single-input multiple-output (SIMO) antenna technique to enhance data demodulation over severe block channel dispersion. In this paper, upper bounds to the bit error probability (BEP) are analytically derived for this GLRT-based SIMO-OFDM system over both deterministic and random block dispersive channels. The BEP analysis is generally applicable to arbitrary two-dimensional component modulations when the SIMO channel consists of deterministic channel paths or independent but not identically distributed random channel paths with a known marginal statistic of CFR magnitudes. The BEP upper bounds are verified by simulation to exhibit tight performance results in pilot-aided SIMO reception, and thus facilitate the error performance characterization of GLRT-based SIMO-OFDM system with respect to data-to-noise power ratio, data-to-pilot power ratio, antenna diversity, and channel condition.
